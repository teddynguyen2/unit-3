Choropleth Visualization of Various Aggregate Data Types in the Autonomous Communities of Spain

Map Summary
The map contains predefined geographic regions of Spain delineated by lines and varying colors, patterned in proportion to variables
such as area, median income, GDP in billions, percentage of GDP, GDP per capita, and HDI that represent an aggregate summary of
statistical data in the particular regions. The user may click on the 19 different autonomous communities of Spain to reveal and
compare the aforementioned data as it varies by geographic region.

The choropleth map legend represents the aggregate summary of statistical data captured for the various variables such as area, median
income, GDP in billions, percentage of GDP, GDP per capita, and HDI in different color shading to delineate greater versus lesser
variable values. That is, greater values will be marked by a darker shade of color, whereas lesser values will be marked by a lighter
shade of color.

A higher rank will be denoted by darker colors in the choropleth map’s legend. That is, greater values are marked by a darker color,
whereas lesser values are marked by a lighter color. These colors span across Spain in the 19 different autonomous communities. The
user may be able to spot, for example, the greatest human development (HDI) is in Madrid, which has a score of 0.928 (darkest shade of
blue); the smallest HDI is in Melilla, which has a score of 0.841 (lightest shade of blue).

Main Findings
Outliers & Anomalies: Ceuta and Melilla are the smallest in terms of area, with an area of 19 and 12 km² respectively. These two cities are
also the smallest in terms of GDP, yet their GDP per capita is roughly equivalent to the cluster of all other autonomous regions in Spain.

Patterns/Trends/Correlations: All autonomous communities do not differ greatly in the median income, GDP per capita, and HDI; however, they
all vary drastically in the total GDP and percentage. One other big difference is that the sizes of the regions vary greatly. There is no
correlation between the size of the autonomous community and its median income, GDP, or even HDI. Regions that generated greater amounts
of revenue tended to be closer to the sea, i.e., Balearic Sea or Mediterranean Sea.

Clusters: Most data clusters near each other, as mentioned in the patterns. The aggregate data cannot be reasonably assumed to correlate with
one another. That is to say, one variable does not correlate with another with statistical significance in this map.
